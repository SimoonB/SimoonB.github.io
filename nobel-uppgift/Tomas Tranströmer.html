Work

Tomas Tranströmer was awarded the Nobel Prize "for his condensed, translucent images, he gives us fresh access to reality," the Swedish Academy's citation.

Even the poet's acclaimed debut collection, 17 Poems (1954), was his style finished an intensely visionary poem, full of precisely chiselled formulations, visually luminous metaphors, archaic mytmotiv and experiences of highly condensed feeling of life. Continuity was evident in the following collections: Secrets of the road (1958), The semi-finished sky (1962) and sounds and grooves (1966). Portrait Poems were common, as are poems with music motives and travel images; history and contemporary life got more and more space.
